# trader-behavior-analysis
Analysis of trader performance using Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear &amp; Greed Index)
# The Psychology of a Trade: How Market Sentiment Shapes Performance

## 📌 The Big Question
We’ve all heard the phrase *"Be fearful when others are greedy,"* but does that actually show up in the data?

This project investigates whether Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear & Greed Index) impacts trader performance on Hyperliquid.

---

## 🔍 The Investigation
This analysis combines two datasets:
- Historical trading data from Hyperliquid (PnL, execution, trade behavior)
- Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index (market sentiment)

Steps performed:
- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Timestamp conversion and alignment
- Merging trading data with sentiment data
- Feature engineering (Profit/Loss classification)
- Exploratory Data Analysis

---

## 📊 What the Data Revealed

### 🚀 Momentum Sweet Spot
- Highest performance during **Extreme Greed**
- Win rate reached ~46%
- Traders perform best in strong bullish momentum

---

### ⚠️ The Fear Paradox
- Traders were more profitable in **Fear markets** than in normal Greed
- Indicates more careful and selective trading behavior

---

### 📉 Neutral Danger Zone
- Lowest performance observed during **Neutral sentiment**
- Lack of clear trend reduces trading efficiency

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
Trading is not just driven by numbers — it is heavily influenced by psychology.

Market sentiment plays a critical role in trader success:
- Extreme Greed → Best performance
- Fear → Strategic opportunities
- Neutral → Weak signals

👉 Sentiment is not noise — it is a measurable edge.
